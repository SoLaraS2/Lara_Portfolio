★ Poems ★

I’ll take the corn


the buttery, glistening smell, you claim to hate

poured from a slender fountain, against all health codes

I chose you to share this moment with, but the tickets are expensive

that first heavenly crunch, you complain hurts your teeth

mouth coated in warmth, you choose to let out in disdain

fingers covered in grease to be sucked on, you brought napkins for

the room at the perfect lighting, we could have done this at home

eyes anywhere but the big screen, we’ve seen this before

the movie I mean

an excuse

to eat the miraculous

irresistible

addictive

heaven sent

piping hot

popped corn.

Have you really ever loved anything like I love popcorn?

Popcorn doesn’t complain, it mingles in my mouth quietly

until I take action upon it

popcorn is always there for me

          I will trade anything for popcorn

Longing for corn


the buttery, glistening smell

losing count of how many 1g salt packs went in

that first crunch you know so well

the room is so dark

only the exit signs glow

air condition set so low

you start to lose your toes

until your dad gives you his jacket

looking like his adorable little packet

fingers covered in grease you can suck on

so many textures of peaceful times

eyes barely on a screen playing anything,

simply an excuse

to eat the miraculous

irresistible

addictive

heaven sent

piping hot

popped corn.

But it is long gone. 

            I would trade anything for one more popcorn

My dream

     You said

Chasing dreams,

    afterthoughts,

forgotten in the morning

    remembered when needed most

no matter how hard you poke,

    will only come back if,

you forget it completely.

    You love them,

so let them go.

    After collecting so many dreams,

putting all the shiny little pieces behind me

    on display for anyone with eyes

anyone that looks like my dreams

    anyone that cares for it

anyone that talks like you

and looks like you

and smells like you

and I-

    chased –all– of my dreams

to look behind me

     and find

        nothing



If I needed to step on my own

    beautiful things

to get here,

    a place without you,

why couldn't anyone have told me

    it hurts more than

it comforts my heart,

    to have the knowledge

the freedom,

    to destroy everything I loved for –one– dream



I close my eyes at night

    barely containing my excitement.

I close my eyes because that is the only way

    I can see you,

that I can almost remember how you said my name.

    My dream.

      Reflection Essay

It’s that time of year again, except this is likely my last as an undergrad, and I can’t decide how I should feel about it. Throughout my time at Mines, I came to understand what true despair means—but definitely not content-wise. Hell, the classes were the easiest part. It was staying alive that truly hurt. Though I don’t think just “hurting” begins to describe it. 

But I am not ungrateful–there were always two lights in my time living here.

One is the walking, breathing and talking inspiration to all my writing, although he will probably never know. It seems everything I write turns into a love story, because all the words I can ever spout, wouldn’t begin to describe what I seem to feel. 

The second light was the given ability to learn how to come as close to describing such a feeling, as words can, allowing me to try, and fail, for the rest of my life.

Creative writing has always been like a tiny stray cat lodged somewhere around my esophagus. I’d sometimes try to cough it out to let it rest elsewhere. Come on, I can’t afford eating AND a cat. 

I have also attempted to fully swallow it, just so it would stop hurting. But the fur and its claws were always against being shoved downwards. 

Your classes have taught me that living with this cat right in the middle, isn’t half bad. Often I feed it new techniques and it grows. Although It may grow to be too big, so I slowly need to open my chest, making more space for the kitty, before I try to pet him again. 

Other times I’d throw it a yarn ball and see how it plays. You never know what it will do with that mouse it found in the corner. He might just kill it. Or adopt it as a new pet. 

I have figured out that the reason I can’t fathom thinking like a writer anymore, is because the kitty already settled into his life here. So he thinks for me. I can no longer read a book without noticing just how many tiny mistakes get through so many editing stages. Or questioning their transition choices. Although me and the kitty can’t help but crave more.

This semester, I have read more than the rest of my whole life combined. And it seems to only be getting worse, as the kitty sees his friends playing with different kinds of toys, and wants to try all of them out. As a proud owner, I couldn't leave it hanging, so I have also written a lot. Although most of it is… subpar. It’s at these revealing moments where I realize the words on the paper shouldn’t have been placed together in that order, that I remember you telling us we need to let ourselves write garbage before anything good comes out. 

But man is it a lot of garbage. 

I have taken to waking up in the middle of my dreams to write down genius ideas (most of which are just… let’s say they were genius to unconscious me!), or taking drives to places I haven’t been to in Colorado, exploring the attractions that no one recommends, to understand how the place feels, and write from their perspectives. 

I don’t know how people feel about this one, but I love sitting in cafes and listing all the traces of the humans around me, hearing some of their conversations and creating scenarios to put them in. How would they respond if this cup of coffee were to suddenly fly across the table? Remember: hypothetical scenarios only! Then I remember Leona Sevick saying her family hates the poems about them, but she writes them anyways. And that makes me comfortable again, after all, it may resemble someone, but it is entirely my perspective of what they are. Poking the little bear on this page won’t hurt the actual one, so it’s okay.

But I think most of all, writing makes me free. I can make my world and drag people into it. 
As fast or as slowly as I wish.
As harshly or softly as your mental bounds will let me.
Keep their minds as close to each Arial letter encoded in binary as I want, or as far as the corner of the bedroom that barely gets any light.

I have known this, and have been slowly working on fixing it, but my greatest downfall is my inability to concatenate. I stick to my own writing biases, which involve extremely short poems paired with extremely long fiction. This may be something I struggle with for the rest of my life, but I have learnt many techniques to at least notice where I can fix it in little steps. 

And for that I can only thank you Seth, for you have pointed out all the potential you can see in my writing, and taught me how to say that something probably needs to be rewritten 15 times before it is barely enjoyable, without hurting anyone’s feelings (this ability has proven useful even in my workplace, more times than I thought I would need to reprimand adults in a lifetime…). But you have also helped me notice when I am down, even if I hadn’t caught on yet. And I recently realized that the more I read and write, the easier it is to read people. So I can only imagine how much you must have read and written over your life to do it so well. Hence, I have been practicing this to translate this deeper understanding of someone into my writing as well.

All this to say, if I ever invent a true hoverboard, you will have one. And if I ever publish any writing, you will be in the acknowledgments, so thank you for making this class, and every other class you teach, the most welcoming environment I could think of in a school full of nerds. I am eternally grateful.
